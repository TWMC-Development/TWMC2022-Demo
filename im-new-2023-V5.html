FAQ

Where do I start?

Welcome to The Woodlands Methodist Church! We are glad you are here. Please take time to explore our website to see all the worship services, Bible studies, classes, outreach activities and events we have to offer. Once you decide to visit one of our three campuses, we hope you will take time to let us know you are here. Stop by Connection Point, located outside each worship venue on The Woodlands Campus, to meet our members, find out more information and receive a gift. If visiting our Woodforest Campus, stop by the First Timer table to connect with church volunteers who are there to help.

What style of worship do you offer?

THE WOODLANDS CAMPUS
We have four distinct worship communities on our campus in The Woodlands.

Traditional Worship
This service takes place in our beautiful Sanctuary. Worship includes favorite hymns, choral and orchestral music, and Christ-centered messages for anyone who longs for timeless traditions.

Contemporary Worship
Our contemporary worship in the Harvest Worship Center is fresh and modern with an uplifting biblical message and soul-satisfying worship music.

Modern Worship
The Loft Worship Center is an inviting renovated warehouse space. This modern worship service offers energetic music and relevant teaching for those who desire a less traditional setting.

Ministerio en Español
Adora con tus vecinos hispanohablantes en un ambiente acogedor en The Woodlands Campus. Nuestro servicio en español está enfocado en la familia con música de adoración contemporánea y una atractiva enseñanza bíblica.

WOODFOREST CAMPUS
Our Woodforest Campus offers family-friendly worship with contemporary music and engaging Bible teaching. Worship with your neighbors in a welcoming worship center that overlooks the natural beauty of the community.

Where do I park?

THE WOODLANDS CAMPUS
We have numerous parking lots convenient to each of our worship communities.

Traditional Worship: Parking lots A, B, C, D, E, F and G are closest to the Sanctuary. Parking lot A is reserved for senior adults and persons requiring special accessibility.

Contemporary Worship: Parking lots K, L, M and N are closest to the Harvest Worship Center.

Modern Worship: Parking lots J, I, P, O, Q, R and S are closest to the Loft Worship Center. Parking lot S is reserved for persons with special needs and persons requiring special accessibility.

Ministerio en Español: Parking lots I, J, K and L are closest to the Recreation Center.

Reference our online maps here.

WOODFOREST CAMPUS
You may enter the parking lot from Woodforest Parkway North or from Fish Creek Thoroughfare. If you have a special need to be close to the building, those parking spots are at the front. On Sundays when our lot is full, you are invited to park across the street in the Stewart Elementary School lot. In instances when we anticipate large overflow parking needs, we typically have golf carts available to safely transport you to the building.

Do you offer child care on Sunday morning?

THE WOODLANDS CAMPUS
Our Noah’s Nook Nursery, for ages 6 weeks through 2 years, is open during worship services in the main building, no reservations necessary. Nursery facilities are also open in the Loft Building. Our nursery is staffed by paid, experienced workers who are trained in the areas of child care, child development, safety and sanitation, and are certified by the American Heart Association in CPR and First Aid.

WOODFOREST CAMPUS
Nursery is available for ages 6 weeks through 2 years. When you arrive, you will see the children’s check-in area. You can sign up your child for nursery at one of the kiosks.

Is programming available for my children on Sunday morning?

THE WOODLANDS CAMPUS

We have a vibrant, active children’s program with classes and activities for children of all ages. In Sunday school, your kids will worship, learn and have fun at their own level. Visit our NextGen Kids page to learn more about what a Sunday morning looks like and what activities we have available for each age-level.

On months with five Sundays, we celebrate 5th Sunday Weekend Family Worship. On Family Worship days, children attend worship services with their families. NextGen Kids staff are on hand with activities for children to get the most out of their worship experience.

WOODFOREST CAMPUS

We worship as a family, inviting all ages to join us. After a time of singing, praying, giving offering and sharing in Communion (the first Sunday of each month), we invite children, preschool through fifth grade, to attend Children’s Church. When you arrive to our building, you will see our children’s check-in area. You can sign up your child for Children’s Church at one of the kiosks.

Do you offer any resources for my child with special needs?

On our campus in The Woodlands, we have an active Special Needs Ministry for children, youth and adults. We offer worship, discipleship and social and recreational opportunities for those with special needs. We also strive to provide care and support for their family members and caregivers.

Do you offer adult Sunday school classes?

Whether you’re a new Christian or you’d like to go deeper into the faith you’ve had for years, we have studies and groups available for all ages, life stages and spiritual journeys, not just on Sunday but throughout the week. Explore our classes and studies to find the fit that’s right for you. Use the filter button to search classes that are specific to your needs.

Is there student ministry on Sunday morning?

THE WOODLANDS CAMPUS
Our student ministry serves students in 5th – 12th grades. Students in 5th and 6th grade meet in the Family Life Center at either 9:30 or 11:00 for Sunday morning worship. Sixth grade students also have the opportunity to participate in Confirmation. Junior high and high school students meet at 11:00 a.m. in the Student Ministry Building for a time of fellowship and worship.

WOODFOREST CAMPUS
The student ministry at Woodforest serves students in grades 6 – 12. Our Sunday morning services are geared for family worship; however, our junior high and high school students have their own additional meeting times. Junior high students gather on Wednesday nights for fellowship, games and interactive Bible study. Senior high students worship God and dive into His Word together in an intimate worship gathering on Sunday nights.

How long is a normal service?

Services in all worship communities on all campuses last about an hour.

Where can I find more information about how TWMC serves the community?

Our church mission statement is Reach People for Jesus Christ, Disciple Them in Faith, and Help Those in Need. That last part — Help Those in Need — is the heartbeat of our church. It is interwoven in and throughout all our ministries and driven by our Missions Ministry. Because we love Jesus Christ, we have hearts of compassion to help those in need.

We have many local and global partners who help us faithfully serve God’s people. Our local partners in Montgomery County, the Houston area and Texas provide opportunities to serve our neighbors. Our global partners work with us to provide mission trip opportunities for us to be the hands and feet of Jesus around the world.

How do I let someone know I have a prayer request?

Prayer is a powerful thing, and we have a committed group of clergy and trusted prayer leaders that will lift up your prayer request throughout the week. Call Caring Ministries at 281.297.5900 or request prayer online.
