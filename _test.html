SUMMARY:

The Woodlands Methodist School is currently seeking a part-time PE Assistant position in our School (PreK – 8th grade) for the 2021-22 school year.

The PE Assistant is a part-time position responsible for supporting the Lead Physical Education Teacher in planning and implementing learning experiences that advance the intellectual, emotional, social and physical development of children within a safe, healthy learning environment. They encourage children to reach their fullest potential, while upholding the Guiding Principles of the school.

The PE Assistant is supervised by a PE Teacher, responsible to the Athletic Director and is also accountable to the Head of School, and TWUMC School Board.

JOB DUTIES:

  • Interact with children
    • Assist with monitoring and instructing children in physical education activities
    • Model positive behavior
    • Assist with children in the class with large group, small groups or individual students
  • Curriculum preparations
    • Assist the Lead Teacher in incorporating required activities into lesson plans that support the children’s development
    • Help Lead Teacher prepare activities that support lesson plans
    • Research, plan, and develop age appropriate activities for lesson plans in conjunction with the Lead Teacher
  • Supervision
    • Assist with carline, and other school-wide duties as assigned
    • Ensure children are supervised at all times, according to the policies of the school and proper ratios are met
  • Administration
    • Inform the Middle School Director or Head of School in cases of unreasonable behavior, parent complaints or other problems which should be handled by administration in the best interest of the school
    • Meet school requirements for in-service training and professional growth in a timely manner
    • Attend monthly staff and team meetings
  • Gym maintenance
    • Assist in caring and maintenance of the gyms, equipment and shared space to ensure a clean and organized learning environment
    • Cooperate with others who share the gyms, including school staff and other TWUMC ministries
  • Other duties, as assigned

REQUIREMENTS:

  • A high school graduate with some college; college degree is preferred
  • Responsible individual with good athletic skills, an interest in working with children, and willingness to work under the supervision of a PE Teacher
  • Ability to maintain confidentiality
  • Flexible, with the ability to work in a highly demanding, stressful environment
  • Excellent oral and written communication and presentation skills
  • Committed Christian who is comfortable sharing their faith with students and families
  • Must meet licensing requirements, which include fingerprinting and an extensive background check

PHYSICAL DEMANDS:

  • The physical demands described here are representative of those that must be met by an employee to successfully perform the essential functions of this job.
  • While performing the duties of this job, the employee is regularly required to talk and hear. This position is very active and requires standing, walking, bending, kneeling, stooping, crouching, crawling, and climbing all day. The employee must be able to lift and/or move items over 30 pounds. Specific vision abilities required by this job include close vision, distance vision, color vision, peripheral vision, depth perception and ability to adjust focus.

CLASSIFICATION:

  • Part time/non-exempt; not to exceed 29 hours/week
  • This position is not benefits eligible.

APPLICATION

All interested applicants should submit a resume and application to TWMS Recruiting (twmsrecruiting@twmc.org). You can find more information on our website (twmschool.org).

Children’s Ministry

DIRECTOR OF CHILDREN’S MINISTRY

The Woodlands Methodist Church is looking for a joy-filled, passionate, full-time children’s ministry director to lead the children’s ministry, 3 years through 6th grade, into a vibrant, faith-filled future. If you are energized by creating a culture focused on ensuring kids learn and experience the gospel of Jesus Christ, and one in which volunteers love to serve, have at least five years of demonstrated experience supervising a staff team and leading a large-scale children’s ministry, have a Bachelor’s degree or more in ministry, education, or related field, and are excited to embrace the challenge of innovating ministry in a post-pandemic world, we’d love to talk with you.

A SAMPLE OF ESSENTIAL FUNCTIONS

Relational Leadership

  • Lead a staff team to create a vibrant environment that allows children to learn and experience the gospel of Jesus on a weekly basis. Provide supervisory oversight and accountability for this team.
  • Create a culture where volunteers love to serve through effective volunteer recruiting, orientation, placement, onboarding, and training.

Strategic Leadership

  • Ensure creation and execution of the children’s ministry vision, values, and goals.
  • Provide ongoing assessment of programs, procedures, and systems to determine effectiveness and alignment. Plan and execute needed changes.

Operational Leadership

  • Prepare and oversee the ministry budget.
  • Ensure health, safety, and evacuation protocols are established, communicated, and executed with all staff and volunteer teams.
  • Work with ministry leaders to ensure clean, inviting, organized, age-appropriate, and safe age-level areas.

Collaboration

  • In partnership with the pastor of marriage and family ministries, create and execute an aligned faith formation plan, birth through graduation.
  • Collaborate with, and supervise the Special Blessings staff ministry team, supporting families of kids with special needs.
  • Be an active member of the Woodlands church ministry staff leadership team.

QUALIFICATIONS

  • A confessing Christian who holds to the Woodland’s statement of faith.
  • Commitment to an ethical lifestyle appropriate for ministry per employee handbook.
  • Minimum of five years demonstrated, effective supervisory ministry leadership experience in a large church setting.
  • Well-rounded biblical background through formal education, or equivalent study and experience. Bachelor’s degree required.
  • Excellent teamwork and management abilities to lead ministry teams, multiple projects, and supervision of employees, volunteers, and contractors.
  • Ability to communicate clearly and effectively through oral and written formats, with a demonstrated ability to make effective group presentations.

OTHER SPECIFICATIONS:

Schedule: As is typical for ministry, this position requires a flexible schedule. It Includes agreed upon onsite office hours, Sunday services, occasional Saturday services, evening activities and meetings, and camps requiring travel

Compensation: Dependent on experience

Benefits: Full benefits, including medical, dental, vision, life, 403b

TO APPLY:

CONFIRMATION
