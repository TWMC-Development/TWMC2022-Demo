Quest Men’s Fellowship

For more than a decade, Quest Men’s Fellowship has been a welcoming space where men of all ages can strengthen their faith and become the men God calls them to be. At Quest, you’ll find a supportive community where you can ask questions, share challenges and encourage one another. By drawing on lessons from the Bible, history and pop culture, Quest not only helps build strong friendships but also guides you in living out your faith as a Godly man.

What to expect?

What to expect?

Quest Men’s Fellowship has both spring and fall sessions within the calendar year. In all sessions, Quest is based on large group teaching either live or via video. Men fellowship and answer discussion questions with their table. Quest utilizes books, and homework/reading varies from semester to semester depending on the study.

Quest offers Sunday evening and Tuesday morning sessions. Registration is not required unless you plan to participate via Zoom.

FALL 2024

Today, many men feel uncertain about what it means to be masculine. More and more, people are talking about the negative effects of toxic masculinity and unfair systems. Because of this, some are holding firmly to old stereotypes, which complicates things even more.

If you’re a man wondering how to use your strength, passions and talents, join Quest this fall. Using Fighting Shadows: Overcoming 7 Lies That Keep Men From Becoming Fully Alive by Jefferson Bethke and Jon Tyson, Quest will challenge men to grow beyond their current limits and lead a life fully devoted to God.

FALL 2020

FALL 2020 - ONE TIME!

Everything is different this fall – Quest included. Our theme is “One Time.” Eight of our pastors will each take a session with the assignment: If you had “one time” to talk to the men of our church, what would you say? Rob Renfroe will kick us off and host all the sessions, but you will have a great chance to hear from many of the best communicators on our staff on some very important topics.

Another difference – Quest will be offered on Zoom only. We surveyed those who have attended in the past and less than 25% wanted to attend in person. We hate not being together, but this “one time” we need to do it this way.
