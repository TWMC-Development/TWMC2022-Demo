Welcome to The Woodlands Methodist Church! We are delighted to have you here. Our church values the importance of embracing visitors and making them feel welcome. Whether you are a long-time churchgoer or someone exploring faith, we believe everyone has a place in our church family.

Please feel free to reach out to any of our church members or staff if you have any questions or if there’s anything we can do to make your time with us more meaningful and fulfilling.

Once again, thank you for choosing to join us in Traditional worship. We hope that you find inspiration, peace and a sense of belonging here at The Woodlands Methodist Church.

Arrival

Our campus has many different entrances. Some might be easier to access depending on your route to campus. The closest entrance to the Sanctuary is Entrance 1 off Lake Woodlands Drive. Click the button below for directions.

Map it

Parking

Parking lots A, B, C, D, E, F and G are closest to the Sanctuary. Parking lot A is reserved for senior adults and persons requiring special accessibility. We hope this makes your walk shorter and your visit a bit easier. Thank you for joining us!

Entrance

The quickest way to get to the Sanctuary for Traditional Worship is through Building Entrance 1. The Connection Point, where you will check in, is at the end of the Outlier Atrium on your left. Someone will be there to greet you.

Check-in

Once you find Connection Point, a volunteer or staff member will get you checked in, guide you anywhere you need to go, and answer any questions you might have. You are then free to enjoy your service, explore our facilities and make new friends. We hope the Plan Your Visit process has been easy and helpful, and we hope you enjoy your visit with us.

Frequently Asked Questions

Where do I start?

Welcome to the Traditional Worship community of The Woodlands Methodist Church! We are glad you are here. Please take time to explore our website to see all the worship services, Bible studies, classes, outreach activities and events we have to offer. Once you decide to visit, stop by Connection Point, located outside the Sanctuary, to meet our members, find out more information and receive a gift.

What style of worship do you offer?

Traditional Worship

This service takes place in our beautiful sanctuary. Worship includes favorite hymns and Christ-centered messages for anyone who longs for timeless traditions.

Where do I park?

Traditional Worship: Parking lots A, B, C, D, E, F and G are closest to the Sanctuary. Parking lot A is reserved for senior adults and persons requiring special accessibility.

Reference our online maps here.

Do you offer child care on Sunday morning?

Our Noah’s Nook Nursery, for ages 6 weeks through 2 years, is open during worship services in the main building, no reservations necessary. Our nursery is staffed by paid, experienced workers who are trained in the areas of child care, child development, safety and sanitation, and are certified by the American Heart Association in CPR and First Aid.

Is programming available for my children on Sunday morning?

We have a vibrant, active children’s program with classes and activities for children of all ages. In Sunday school, your kids will worship, learn and have fun at their own level. Visit our NextGen Kids page to learn more about what a Sunday morning looks like and what activities we have available for each age-level.

On months with five Sundays, we celebrate 5th Sunday Weekend Family Worship. On Family Worship days, children attend worship services with their families. NextGen Kids staff are on hand with activities for children to get the most out of their worship experience.

Do you offer any resources for my child with special needs?

We have an active Special Needs Ministry for children, youth and adults located in the Loft Building. We offer worship, discipleship and social and recreational opportunities for those with special needs. We also strive to provide care and support for their family members and caregivers.

Do you offer adult Sunday school classes?

Whether you’re a new Christian or you’d like to go deeper into the faith you’ve had for years, we have studies and groups available for all ages, life stages and spiritual journeys, not just on Sunday but throughout the week. Explore our classes and studies to find the fit that’s right for you. Use the filter button to search classes that are specific to your needs.

Is there student ministry on Sunday morning?

Our student ministry serves students in 5th – 12th grades. Students in 5th and 6th grade meet in the Family Life Center at either 9:30 or 11:00 for Sunday morning worship. Sixth grade students also have the opportunity to participate in Confirmation. Junior high and high school students meet at 11:00 a.m. in the Student Ministry Building for a time of fellowship and worship.

How long is a normal service?

Traditional Worship services last about an hour.

Where can I find more information about how TWMC serves the community?

Our church mission statement is Reach People for Jesus Christ, Disciple Them in Faith, and Help Those in Need. That last part — Help Those in Need — is the heartbeat of our church. It is interwoven in and throughout all our ministries and driven by our Missions Ministry. Because we love Jesus Christ, we have hearts of compassion to help those in need.

We have many local and global partners who help us faithfully serve God’s people. Our local partners in Montgomery County, the Houston area and Texas provide opportunities to serve our neighbors . Our global partners work with us to provide mission trip opportunities for us to be the hands and feet of Jesus around the world.

How do I let someone know I have a prayer request?

Prayer is a powerful thing, and we have a committed group of clergy and trusted prayer leaders that will lift up your prayer request throughout the week. Call Caring Ministries at 281.297.5900 or request prayer online.
