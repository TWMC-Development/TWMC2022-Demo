The Woodlands Methodist Church has always been a traditional, Wesleyan congregation with a three-part mission: win people to Jesus Christ, disciple them in faith, and help those in need. TWMC has not wavered from this mission in the four-plus decades since its inception in 1978. Since that time, TWMC has steadily grown in membership and attendance to become one of the largest congregations in Methodism.

Member Survey

We are committed to gathering all the information necessary to develop the most effective plan for advancing the ministries of this church now and for decades to come. Member participation is a vitally important part of the discernment process. For this reason, church members are invited to give input by completing our member survey.

The member survey is presented in a simple, digital-only format and takes approximately five minutes to complete. Survey results will be compiled and shared with other discernment findings in the near future.

Discernment Frameworks

There are many factors to consider as the church discerns future affiliation and embarks on a new, post-UMC era of ministry; however, none are more important to the decision-making process than assessment of options in comparison to the foundational pillars that give unique form and purpose to the ministries of The Woodlands Methodist Church.

With the persistent goal of preserving the God-inspired, 44-year-old identity of The Woodlands Methodist Church, its executive leadership team has developed the list of six fundamental and distinctive criteria — what we call discernment frameworks — that will serve as the measurement tool by which all future affiliation options will be compared. • Theology

Today we are working to cultivate the deeply rooted heritage of Christian theology known as Wesleyan Methodism. While principles and practices vary greatly among denominations within the world of Methodism; The Woodlands Methodist Church upholds the traditional, orthodox versions of Wesleyan doctrine and discipline such as scriptural holiness as described by Methodism’s founder, John Wesley.

We believe the historic Christian faith, as its documented Holy Scripture, offers a transforming relationship with the living God through Jesus Christ in the power of the Holy Spirit. It cannot be replaced, reinvented or re-written by individuals that seek to make their own version of personal truth, morality, and understanding of righteousness.
